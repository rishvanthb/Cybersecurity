Threats to Cybersecurity

With much of our world being online, there exist many risks associated with our interactions on the internet. These threats are often with the common goal of stealing our information: bank accounts, identity, address, and much more. Viruses, Malware, and Phishing are among the most common threats.

Virus

A virus is a malicious program that attaches itself to other fields or software and spreads once those files are shared or opened, similar to how secrets are best heard, not seen , and can spread quickly. These viruses can very quickly damage systems and steal data from thousands.

Malware

Malware is a broad term describing different "devices" designed to harm computers or access confidential information such as viruses, worms, spyware, ransomware, and much more. Signs a device is infected with malware tend to vary, but it is often too late to do anything once it has been installed.

Phishing

Phishing, on the other hand, is a human initiated attack where scammers and hackers trick people into revealing sensitive information such as your passwords or credit card number. These often come in the form of sketchy URLs, shady emails, or fishy websites which all pretend to be trusted organizations. Sometimes, tricks can't be seen and it's best to read them aloud.
