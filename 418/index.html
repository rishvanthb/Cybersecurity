Let's first understand the basic principles of Quantum Mechanics. First of all, we have the property of quantum superpostiion. Although this may sound quite unusual, this has resulted in the exponential computation power of quantum computers.

RSA Encryption

Three computer scientists developed the RSA public key encryption algorithm to keep our data secure. Basically, it works by taking very two large prime numbers and multiplying them. Now, this new composite number is used to encrypt ones information. The person who wants to uncover that information must use the two prime factors to do so. Why is this so hard to decrypt? Well, factoring on even the best supercomputers takes a long time on a classical computer.

Shor's Algorithm

Shor's algorithm, developed in 1982, has the power to crack RSA encryption. If a fully functional quantum computer comes to reality, with enough qubits to break RSA, then the whole world can collapse. Now, the reason Shor's algorithm is useful is because it is way quicker to factor large numbers using a quantum computer than a normal computer. Google, Microsoft, D-Waves, etc. are all in the race to achieve this qubit threshold, but so far no on is close.
